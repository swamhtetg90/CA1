Don't Breathe Air: It's Deadly!

Protecting Our Future: The Urgent Need for Clean Energy

Do you know that 5 million people die every year due to air pollution caused by burning fossil fuels? This is more than half of the 8 million deaths attributed to air pollution globally. The extensive use of non-renewable resources like coal and oil is creating an alarming health crisis.

According to a study published by BMJ, many of these deaths could have been avoided if we had never relied on fossil fuels. The evidence is clear: reducing our dependency on these harmful energy sources is directly linked to a healthier, safer population.

In a comprehensive study, researchers from around the world examined the impact of air pollution on human health. Their findings confirmed a strong link between fossil fuel use and atmospheric pollution. This reinforces the urgent need to shift towards cleaner, renewable energy sources.

Transitioning to renewable energy not only saves lives but also promotes a more sustainable world. This is why the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goal 7 (SDG 7) is so critical. It aims to ensure access to affordable, reliable, sustainable, and modern energy for all, targeting to increase substantially the share of renewable energy in the global energy mix as in target 7.2.

Although air pollution remains a major threat to many countries, some nations such as Costa Rica and Germany have begun to utilise renewable energy resources to address the issue, aligning with UN SDG 7. Costa Rica has been a global leader in carbon neutrality for the past seven years. Since 2014, the country has generated over 98% of its electricity from renewable sources, making it one of the greenest nations on the planet. In 2021, Costa Rica continued its efforts to remain carbon neutral, aiming to produce over 99% of its energy from renewable resources. This commitment showcases Costa Rica's dedication to sustainability and serves as an inspiring model for other countries.

Germany's energy transition, known as the Energiewende, has transformed from a grassroots movement into a national project with profound effects on society and business. Germany aims to phase out coal by 2038, with new climate targets suggesting an even earlier exit. Germany's goal is to power its industry, mobility, and buildings almost entirely with renewables, proving that an industrialized nation can abandon fossil fuels without sacrificing growth.

Costa Rica and Germany's achievements demonstrate that transitioning to renewable energy is both possible and beneficial, inspiring each individual and nation worldwide to follow the path set by UN SDG 7.
